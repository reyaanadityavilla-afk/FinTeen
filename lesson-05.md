# LESSON 5: FINANCIAL MARKETS

## Introduction

Imagine you have:

₹10,000

that you want to invest.

At the same time:

A company needs money to expand.

A government needs money to build roads.

A startup needs funding to hire employees.

How does money move from people who have money to people who need money?

The answer is:

**Financial Markets.**

Financial Markets are the systems that connect:

* Investors
* Businesses
* Governments
* Banks

Financial Markets are one of the most important parts of the global economy.

Every day, trillions of dollars move through financial markets around the world.

These markets determine:

* Stock prices
* Interest rates
* Investment returns
* Company valuations
* Economic growth

Without financial markets, modern economies could not function efficiently.

Understanding financial markets is essential because they form the foundation of investing, banking, corporate finance, and investment banking.

---

# What Are Financial Markets?

Financial Markets are places where buyers and sellers exchange financial assets.

In simple words:

Financial markets allow money and investments to be bought and sold.

Examples of financial assets:

* Stocks
* Bonds
* Currencies
* Commodities
* Derivatives

---

# Why Financial Markets Exist

Financial Markets help move money efficiently.

People with extra money:

Investors.

People who need money:

Businesses and governments.

Financial markets connect both sides.

This process is called:

### Capital Allocation

Efficient capital allocation helps economies grow.

---

# Example

Investor:

Has ₹1,00,000.

Company:

Needs ₹1,00,000 for expansion.

Financial markets connect them.

The company gets funding.

The investor gets ownership or returns.

Both benefit.

---

# Functions of Financial Markets

Financial markets perform several important functions.

---

# Raising Capital

Companies raise money.

Examples:

* Selling shares
* Issuing bonds

Financial markets make this possible.

---

# Liquidity

Liquidity means the ability to buy or sell assets quickly.

Example:

If you own shares of a public company, you can often sell them immediately.

This flexibility attracts investors.

---

# Price Discovery

Financial markets determine prices.

Examples:

* Stock prices
* Bond prices
* Currency values

Prices are determined through supply and demand.

---

# Risk Transfer

Investors can choose different risk levels.

Examples:

* Government bonds
* Stocks
* Derivatives

Financial markets help distribute risk.

---

# Economic Growth

Efficient markets support business growth.

Businesses can raise money more easily.

This leads to:

* Innovation
* Jobs
* Productivity

---

# Primary Markets

There are two major market types.

The first is:

### Primary Market

This is where new securities are created.

---

# Example

A company conducts an IPO.

New shares are sold to investors.

Money goes directly to the company.

This occurs in the primary market.

---

# Secondary Markets

After securities are issued, they trade among investors.

This is called the:

### Secondary Market

---

# Example

You buy Apple shares from another investor.

Apple does not receive the money.

The transaction occurs between investors.

This is a secondary market transaction.

---

# Stock Market

One of the most famous financial markets.

---

# What Is the Stock Market?

The stock market allows investors to buy and sell shares of companies.

When you buy a share:

You become a partial owner.

---

# Examples

Stock exchanges include:

* NSE
* BSE
* NYSE
* NASDAQ

Thousands of companies trade on these exchanges.

---

# Why Companies Issue Shares

Companies issue shares to:

* Raise money
* Fund growth
* Expand operations

Investors provide capital in exchange for ownership.

---

# Why Investors Buy Shares

Investors expect:

* Capital appreciation
* Dividends
* Long-term wealth creation

Stocks have historically generated strong long-term returns.

---

# Bond Market

The bond market is another major financial market.

---

# What Is a Bond?

A bond is a loan made by investors to governments or companies.

Instead of buying ownership, investors lend money.

---

# Example

Investor buys:

₹10,000 bond.

Government promises:

Interest payments.

Plus repayment later.

---

# Bond Market Importance

Governments and corporations raise huge amounts of money through bonds.

The global bond market is larger than the global stock market.

---

# Money Market

Money markets deal with short-term financial instruments.

Usually:

Less than one year.

Examples:

* Treasury Bills
* Commercial Paper
* Certificates of Deposit

These investments are generally lower risk.

---

# Foreign Exchange Market (Forex)

The Forex Market is where currencies trade.

Examples:

* USD
* INR
* EUR
* GBP
* JPY

It is the largest financial market in the world.

---

# Example

You exchange:

Indian Rupees

for

US Dollars.

This transaction occurs in the forex market.

---

# Commodity Markets

Commodities are physical goods.

Examples:

* Gold
* Silver
* Oil
* Wheat
* Natural Gas

Commodity markets allow trading of these resources.

---

# Why Commodity Markets Matter

Commodity prices affect:

* Inflation
* Business costs
* Economic growth

Oil prices alone influence economies worldwide.

---

# Derivatives Market

One of the most advanced financial markets.

---

# What Is a Derivative?

A derivative is a financial contract whose value depends on another asset.

Examples:

* Stock options
* Futures contracts
* Swaps

Derivatives are often used for:

* Hedging
* Speculation
* Risk management

---

# Individual Investors

* Retirees

---

# Institutional Investors

Examples:

# Banks

# Supply and Demand

Financial markets operate on supply and demand.

---

# Example

More Buyers than Sellers:

Price rises.

---

More Sellers than Buyers:

Price falls.

This simple principle drives market prices.

---

# Bull Markets

A Bull Market occurs when prices generally rise.

Characteristics:

* Investor optimism
* Economic growth
* Strong corporate earnings

Bull markets often create wealth.

---

# Bear Markets

A Bear Market occurs when prices generally fall.

Characteristics:

* Investor fear
* Economic weakness
* Lower confidence

Bear markets can create opportunities for long-term investors.

---

# Market Capitalization

Market Cap measures company value.

Formula:

### Market Cap = Share Price × Shares Outstanding

---

# Example

Share Price:

₹100

Shares Outstanding:

10 Lakh

Market Cap:

₹10 Crore

Market capitalization is widely used by investors.

---

# Market Efficiency

Market efficiency refers to how quickly information affects prices.

Efficient markets incorporate new information rapidly.

This concept is central to modern finance.

---

# Financial Markets and the Economy

Financial markets often reflect economic conditions.

Strong economy:

Weak economy:

Markets often struggle.

However, markets can sometimes move ahead of economic changes.
