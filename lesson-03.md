# LESSON 3: PRIVATE EQUITY (PE)

## Introduction

Imagine there are two companies.

### Company A

A brand-new startup.

Only 10 employees.

No profits yet.

Huge growth potential.

This company would likely attract:

**Venture Capitalists**

---

### Company B

50 years old.

Thousands of employees.

Profitable.

Strong customer base.

However, management is inefficient and growth has slowed.

This company would likely attract:

**Private Equity Firms**

Private Equity is one of the most prestigious and highest-paying fields in finance.

Private Equity firms buy companies, improve them, grow them, and eventually sell them for a profit.

Many billion-dollar companies have been transformed through private equity ownership.

Understanding Private Equity is important because it combines:

* Investing
* Finance
* Business Strategy
* Corporate Management
* Valuation

Private Equity is often considered one of the most powerful sectors in global finance.

---

# What Is Private Equity?

Private Equity (PE) is the business of investing in private companies or buying public companies and making them private.

In simple words:

Private Equity firms buy businesses, improve them, and sell them later for more money.

The goal is to increase company value.

---

# What Does "Private" Mean?

A private company is not traded on a public stock exchange.

Examples:

Not listed on:

* NSE
* BSE
* NYSE
* NASDAQ

Ownership belongs to:

* Founders
* Investors
* Private owners

Private Equity focuses heavily on these businesses.

---

# How Private Equity Works

The process is relatively simple.

### Step 1

Identify a company.

---

### Step 2

Buy the company.

---

### Step 3

Improve operations.

---

### Step 4

Increase profits.

---

### Step 5

Sell the company.

---

### Step 6

Earn investment returns.

---

The goal of Private Equity is simple:

Buy low.

Improve.

Sell high.

PE firms try to increase company value over several years.

# Why Companies Need PE Firms

Many businesses have problems.

Examples:

Private Equity firms often help solve these problems.

# What PE Firms Actually Do

Many people think PE firms only invest money.

That is incorrect.

Private Equity firms are often highly involved.

They may:

* Hire new executives
* Improve operations
* Cut costs
* Expand internationally
* Acquire competitors

They actively create value.

---

# Venture Capital vs Private Equity

This is one of the most important distinctions in finance.

---

# Venture Capital

Invests in startups.

Usually:

* Small companies
* Early-stage businesses
* High risk

Example:

Investing in FinTeen when it has:

1,000 users.

---

# Private Equity

Invests in mature companies.

Usually:

* Established businesses
* Existing profits
* Larger operations

Example:

Investing in FinTeen after it becomes a nationwide company.

---

# Risk Levels

Generally:

### Venture Capital

Higher Risk

Higher Potential Reward

---

### Private Equity

Lower Risk

More Predictable Returns

---

# Leveraged Buyouts (LBOs)

One of the most famous PE strategies.

---

# What Is an LBO?

LBO stands for:

Leveraged Buyout.

A company is purchased using a significant amount of borrowed money.

---

# Example

Company Value:

₹1,000 Crore

PE Firm Invests:

₹200 Crore

Borrowed Money:

₹800 Crore

Total Purchase Price:

₹1,000 Crore

The acquisition is heavily financed through debt.

---

# Why Use Debt?

Debt allows PE firms to control larger companies while investing less of their own money.

If the company grows successfully:

Returns can be much larger.

---

# Risks of LBOs

Debt creates risk.

If the company struggles:

Repaying debt becomes difficult.

This can lead to financial problems.

---
