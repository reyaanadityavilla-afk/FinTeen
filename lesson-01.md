# LESSON 1: INVESTMENT BANKING

## Introduction

Imagine you are the CEO of a company.

Your business is growing rapidly.

You want to:

* Raise ₹500 Crore
* Buy another company
* Sell your company
* Go public on the stock market

These are extremely important decisions involving huge amounts of money.

Who helps companies make these decisions?

Investment Bankers.

Investment Banking is one of the most prestigious, competitive, and highest-paying careers in the world of finance.

Investment bankers work on transactions worth millions, billions, and sometimes even trillions of dollars.

They advise:

* Governments
* Corporations
* Startups
* Billionaires
* Institutional Investors

Investment banking sits at the center of global finance.

Understanding investment banking helps explain how major businesses raise money, expand, merge, and grow.

---

# What Is Investment Banking?

Investment Banking is a financial service that helps companies, governments, and organizations raise money and complete major financial transactions.

In simple words:

Investment bankers help businesses make important financial decisions.

Examples include:

* Raising capital
* Selling companies
* Buying companies
* Going public
* Issuing bonds

---

# What Do Investment Bankers Actually Do?

Many people think investment bankers simply invest money.

This is incorrect.

Investment bankers are advisors.

They help clients make financial decisions.

Their work generally falls into several major areas.

---

# Raising Capital

One of the biggest responsibilities.

Businesses often need money.

Examples:

* Opening factories
* Expanding internationally
* Hiring employees
* Building products

Investment bankers help companies raise this money.

---

# Debt Financing

Companies borrow money.

Examples:

* Loans
* Bonds

Investment bankers help structure and sell these debt instruments.

---

# Equity Financing

Companies sell ownership.

Examples:

* Shares
* Stock offerings

Investment bankers help attract investors.

---

# Initial Public Offering (IPO)

One of the most famous investment banking activities.

---

# What Is an IPO?

IPO stands for:

Initial Public Offering.

It occurs when a private company becomes publicly traded.

Example:

Before IPO:

Only founders and investors own shares.

After IPO:

Anyone can buy shares on the stock market.

---

# Why Companies Go Public

Companies go public to:

* Raise money
* Expand operations
* Increase visibility
* Provide liquidity to investors

Investment banks manage the entire process.

---

# Example

Imagine FinTeen becomes a huge company.

Years later it decides to raise:

₹1,000 Crore

through an IPO.

Investment bankers would:

* Value the company
* Prepare financial documents
* Market shares to investors
* Determine pricing

This is investment banking.

---

# Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A)

Another major investment banking activity.

---

# What Is a Merger?

A merger occurs when two companies combine.

Example:

Company A + Company B

become

One larger company.

---

# What Is an Acquisition?

An acquisition occurs when one company buys another.

Example:

Microsoft buys a software company.

The acquired company becomes part of Microsoft.

---

# Role of Investment Bankers in M&A

Investment bankers:

* Find buyers
* Find sellers
* Negotiate prices
* Analyze risks
* Structure deals

Many M&A transactions are worth billions.

---

# Example

Company Value:

₹5,000 Crore

Acquisition Price:

₹6,000 Crore

Investment bankers help determine whether the deal makes financial sense.

---

# Corporate Advisory

Investment bankers advise executives on:

* Growth strategies
* Capital allocation
* Expansion
* Financial restructuring

Their expertise helps companies make informed decisions.

---

# Financial Modeling

One of the most important investment banking skills.

---

# What Is Financial Modeling?

Financial modeling involves building spreadsheets that predict company performance.

Analysts forecast:

* Revenue
* Expenses
* Profit
* Cash Flow

These models support decision-making.

---

# Example

An investment banker may forecast:

Revenue for the next:

5 years

before recommending an acquisition.

---

# Valuation

Investment bankers constantly determine company value.

Questions include:

* What is this company worth?
* What should investors pay?
* Is the acquisition price fair?

Valuation is a core investment banking skill.

---

# Pitch Books

Investment bankers create presentations called pitch books.

These documents help persuade clients.

Pitch books often contain:

* Industry analysis
* Valuation
* Recommendations
* Financial projections

Pitch books are central to banking work.

---

# Types of Investment Banks

---

# Bulge Bracket Banks

The largest investment banks in the world.

Examples include:

* Goldman Sachs
* Morgan Stanley
* JPMorgan
* Bank of America

These firms handle massive transactions.

---

# Middle Market Banks

Smaller than global banks.

Focus on mid-sized companies.

---

# Boutique Banks

Specialized firms.

Often focus on:

* Mergers
* Acquisitions
* Specific industries

---

# Investment Banking Hierarchy

Most banks follow a similar structure.

---

# Analyst

Entry-level role.

Responsibilities:

* Financial modeling
* Research
* Excel work
* Presentation preparation

Many analysts work long hours.

---

# Associate

Manages analysts.

Handles more client work.

---

# Vice President

Manages transactions.

Coordinates teams.

---

# Director

Builds client relationships.

Generates business.

---

# Managing Director

Senior executive.

Responsible for winning major clients and deals.

---

# Why Investment Banking Pays So Much

Investment banking is one of the highest-paying careers.

Reasons include:

* High responsibility
* Long hours
* Complex work
* Large transactions

Top bankers can earn enormous compensation.

---

# Typical Skills Needed

Successful investment bankers develop:

* Accounting
* Finance
* Valuation
* Excel
* PowerPoint
* Communication
* Analytical thinking

These skills are heavily emphasized at top business schools.

---

# Investment Banking and Accounting

Investment bankers must understand:

* Income Statements
* Balance Sheets
* Cash Flow Statements

Nearly every deal requires financial analysis.

---

# Investment Banking and Valuation

Bankers constantly answer:

"What is this company worth?"

Valuation drives:

* Acquisitions
* IPOs
* Investments

This makes valuation one of the most important banking skills.

---

# Investment Banking and Startups

Startups often work with investment banks when:

* Raising large amounts of money
* Going public
* Being acquired

Many successful startups eventually enter the investment banking ecosystem.

---

Revenue:

₹100 Crore

Users:

10 Million

An investment bank might help FinTeen:

* Raise capital
* Expand globally

This demonstrates how investment bankers support growing businesses.

* High compensation
* Strong career opportunities
* Exposure to major businesses
* Valuable financial skills
* Prestigious profession

---

# Challenges of Investment Banking

* Long working hours
* High pressure
* Competitive environment

The career is rewarding but challenging.

# Why Investment Banking Matters

Investment bankers help allocate capital efficiently.

They connect:

* Investors
* Businesses
* Governments

This supports economic growth and business expansion.

Without investment banks, many major financial transactions would be far more difficult.

Investment banking combines:

* Accounting

* Corporate Finance
* Financial Markets
* Business Strategy
